# Operating Systems Synchronization

## Why this topic was chosen

Operating Systems synchronization was chosen because it is one of the clearest examples of a topic where students repeatedly fail due to conceptual misunderstanding rather than lack of exposure.

Common struggle areas include:

- mutex vs semaphore
- deadlock conditions
- starvation vs deadlock
- producer-consumer logic
- reader-writer coordination
- race condition identification

This makes it a strong fit for a system that diagnoses misconceptions and recommends targeted interventions.

## Why it fits CogniWeave well

This topic is useful for the project because:

- the concept graph is rich
- misconceptions are stable and recognizable
- state-tracing interventions are natural
- progress can be evaluated through scenario-based reasoning

Examples of possible interventions:

- semaphore state trace
- deadlock-condition classification
- compare similar synchronization scenarios
- fill-in-the-missing-step exercise
- identify why a given solution still causes a race condition

## What this pack proves

If the system works on OS synchronization, it shows that the same architecture can handle topics that depend more on systems reasoning and state transitions than on procedural practice alone.

That makes the project look broader and more serious than a single-domain tutor.

## Why it matters for engineering students

Synchronization is a classic engineering-student pain point in core CS, especially in OS courses, interviews, and systems preparation.

It gives the project a strong CS-theory and systems flavor.
